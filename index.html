I'm a mechanical engineer-in-training driven by a passion for building things that matter — from restoring a 1983 BMW chassis to designing a wearable medical device at Worcester Polytechnic Institute.

Based in Austin, Texas, I'm currently pursuing dual studies at Harmony School of Endeavor and Austin Community College (GPA 4.56), while leading real-world engineering projects across automotive restoration, medical R&D, and software development.

My work spans mechanical design in Fusion 360 & AutoCAD, embedded systems programming in C++, Python, and JavaScript, and hands-on fabrication. Whether I'm disassembling a decades-old engine or writing firmware for a biosensor, I bring the same mindset: understand the system deeply, then build something better.

Outside of engineering, I climb. One month in, I hold my lead climbing certification and am already projecting 5.10d sport routes, 5.11c on top rope, and V7 boulder problems. The mountains aren't just a theme here — K2 is my favorite for a reason. The same discipline that separates a summit attempt from the summit is what I bring to every build. I'm fluent in Turkish and English, grounded in both cultures, and always chasing the next route worth sending.
